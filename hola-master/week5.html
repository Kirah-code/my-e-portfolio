Narrative Report

Date: May 30, 2025

Location: Online Meeting via MS Teams

Demo Presenters: Andrea Mae, Cortez, Danica Mae Federio, Mark Arjen Magno, Andy Boy Estipular

Observers: Prof. Kristel May Alvarado

Introduction

During the fifth week, our class conducted initial online demonstration teaching sessions via Google Meet. Each group was assigned to deliver a micro-teaching session focusing on selected common competencies in Home Economics. This served as a foundational exercise where we, as student teachers, could apply our lesson planning and teaching techniques in an online environment. I took the role of an observer, attentively watching how my peers introduced their topics, delivered content, and utilized visual and digital tools to engage learners. Feedback from the observer emphasized that while most presenters had a good grasp of their content, there was a need to improve time management and visual material design. It was also recommended to add interactive elements such as quizzes or polling to enhance student engagement. From this, I learned the importance of rehearsing for proper pacing, designing more visually consistent presentations using tools like Canva or PowerPoint animations, and beginning each session with short yet meaningful attention-grabbing activities.

Observers’ Feedback

Ms. Villanueva praised the presenters' clarity and composure throughout the session. Mr. Santos noted that the activity demonstrated the students’ readiness to serve as peer facilitators and contribute to the university’s mental health initiatives.
