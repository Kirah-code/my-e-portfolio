Narrative Report

Event Title: Peer Wellness Awareness Session

Date: July 18, 2025

Location: TUP-Taguig Guidance Office AVR

Presenters: Ariane Reyes, Joshua De Leon, Bianca Torres

Observers: Ms. Elena Villanueva (Guidance Counselor), Mr. Leo Santos (PFG Adviser)

Introduction

Week 9 marked my official live online teaching demonstration. My assigned topic was Controlling Hazards and Risks in Commercial Cooking, a core competency under the TLE 7 curriculum. To prepare, I designed a comprehensive Daily Lesson Plan (DLP), developed a multimedia PowerPoint presentation, and created a short formative quiz using Google Forms. I began the session with a short situational video showing real-life kitchen accidents to grab the students' attention. I then presented and discussed the three main types of hazards—biological, physical, and chemical—using infographics and everyday kitchen examples. I maintained learner engagement through questioning strategies and facilitated a post-lesson discussion based on quiz responses. Observer feedback was mostly positive, highlighting my effective use of real-life examples, visual materials, and a student-friendly approach. However, I was encouraged to improve my transitions between topics and to pause longer after asking questions to give learners more time to respond. I was also advised to reinforce the conclusion by summarizing key points and incorporating safety posters or symbols in my visuals for better retention. This experience allowed me to apply classroom management strategies even in a virtual environment and built my confidence in live instructional delivery.

Observers’ Feedback

Ms. Villanueva praised the presenters' clarity and composure throughout the session. Mr. Santos noted that the activity demonstrated the students’ readiness to serve as peer facilitators and contribute to the university’s mental health initiatives.
